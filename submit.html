Submission Guidelines

We welcome research papers, critical essays, policy memos, creative scholarship, and multimedia storytelling from high school and undergraduate contributors worldwide. Every submission receives two rounds of peer review paired with mentor feedback.

How to submit

Email your manuscript and abstract to rmohamed@dohacollege.com.qa.

No AI-written submissions. We only publish work authored and edited by real students. Pieces generated or heavily drafted by AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) are rejected immediately and may result in a full ban from future issues.
Checklist

What to prepare

Manuscript

500+ words (essays and articles) or up to 12 pages (research). Use APA, MLA, or Chicago style.

Abstract

150-word summary with keywords, methodology snapshot, and why the topic matters now.

Artifacts

Optional: data visualizations, artwork, audio clips, or lesson plans to accompany your piece.

FAQ

Contributor questions

Who can submit?

Any student ages 14–22. Joint submissions and campus lab teams welcome.

Do I need a teacher sponsor?

No, but we encourage looping in a mentor to help with data ethics and permissions.

What rights do I retain?

You keep ownership. We publish under CC BY-NC-SA and provide DOI registration.

Can I submit previously published work?

We accept revised conference papers or blog essays if you hold rights to republish.
